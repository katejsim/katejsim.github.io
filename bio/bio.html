BIO

I am a PhD Candidate at the Oxford Internet Institute where I research the gendered dimensions of emerging technologies.

My dissertation project researches the automation and datafication of sexual harassment reporting procedures in US higher education. I employ ethnographically-informed methods to uncover the power relations embedded in the design and integration of reporting tools. Previously, I studied the practical and ethical scopes of predictive risk assessment tools in UK child protection services, and examined feminist politics of consent and sexuality.

I have nearly a decade of experience in community organizing, anti-rape advocacy, and survivor support in the US, UK, and South Korea. I am incredibly proud to have been a part of youth- and survivor-led campaigns like Know Your IX and Our Harvard Can Do Better to demand a safer and just campus. The lessons in community building, accountability and justice, and solidarity continue to inform my work.

I hold a MSc from the Oxford Internet Institute and a BA in gender and sexuality studies from Harvard.
