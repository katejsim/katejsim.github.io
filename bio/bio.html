BIO

Kate Sim (she/hers) is a PhD Candidate at the Oxford Internet Institute examining the intersection of gender, technology, and social justice.

Her dissertation project researches the automation and datafication of sexual harassment reporting procedures in US higher education. She employs ethnographically-informed methods to uncover power relations encoded in the design and integration of data- and AI-driven reporting systems. Previously, Kate researched the ethical and practical scopes of predictive risk assessment tools for child protection services in the UK, and the sexual politics of consent discourse.

Kate has nearly a decade of experience in community organizing, survivor advocacy, and qualitative research in US, UK, and South Korea. She is incredibly proud to have been a part of youth- and survivor-led campaigns like Know Your IX and Our Harvard Can Do Better to demand a safer and just campus. The lessons in community building, accountability and justice, and solidarity continue to inform her work.

She holds a MSc on Social Science of the Internet from the Oxford Internet Institute and a BA on Gender and Sexuality Studies from Harvard.
