ABOUT

I am a qualitative researcher studying the intersection of gender, technology, and justice. My PhD research at the Oxford Internet Institute examines the ethical, legal, and sociocultural implications of data- and AI-driven reporting tools for sexual harassment. Much of my work takes ethnographically-informed approaches to uncover the power relations encoded in the design and integration of new technologies.

My research interests span across: feminist epistemology; sexual violence; algorithmic help-seeking and decision-making; consent and intimacy; affect and design in human-computer interaction (HCI); social control and power; qualitative methods.

You can reach me at kate.sim [at] oii.ox.ac.uk.
