ABOUT

I am a qualitative researcher studying the intersection of gender, technology, and justice. My PhD research at the Oxford Internet Institute examines the ethical, legal, and sociopolitical implications of data- and AI-driven reporting systems for sexual violence. Much of my work takes ethnographically-informed approaches to uncover the power automated and datafied systems. I collaborate closely with survivors, practitioners, and technologists to inform and guide my research.

I hold a MSc on social science from the Oxford Internet Institute and a BA in gender and sexuality studies from Harvard.

You can reach me at kate.sim@oii.ox.ac.uk and @katejsim on Twitter.
